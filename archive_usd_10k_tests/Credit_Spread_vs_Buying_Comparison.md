## Strategy Details

### Selling Premium (Credit Spreads)

**What We Did:**
- Sold **Bull Put Spreads** when neutral/bullish
- Sold **Bear Call Spreads** when neutral/bearish
- Spread width: $10
- Strikes: 3% OTM (out-of-the-money)
- DTE: 30 days at entry

**How It Works:**

**Bull Put Spread Example:**
```
SPY trading at $680
1. Sell $660 put (collect $4 premium)
2. Buy $650 put (pay $1 premium)
Net credit: $3 per spread

Max Profit: $3 (if SPY stays above $660)
Max Loss: $7 (if SPY drops below $650)
Risk/Reward: $7 risk to make $3 (need 70% win rate to profit)

Result: 88.2% win rate exceeds 70% threshold!
```

**Exit Rules:**
- ‚úÖ Close at 50% profit (spread worth $1.50 ‚Üí keep $1.50, return $8.50 collateral)
- ‚ùå Close at 200% loss (spread worth $6 ‚Üí lose $3)
- ‚è∞ Close at 21 days max hold

**Why It Won:**
1. **Theta decay works FOR us** - every day spread loses value = we profit
2. **High probability** - only need stock to not crash (easier than predicting direction)
3. **Quick exits** - average 1.8 days to 50% profit
4. **Range-bound market** - perfect for sellers (stock didn't move much)

## When NOT to Use Credit Spreads

Credit spreads can lose money in:

‚ùå **Strong Trending Markets**
- Example: Stock rallies 20% in 2 months
- Bear call spreads get run over
- Better strategy: Buying calls or covered calls

‚ùå **High Volatility Crashes**
- Example: Market drops 10% in one week
- Bull put spreads go max loss
- Better strategy: Long puts or no position

‚ùå **Earnings Announcements**
- Example: Stock moves 15% overnight
- Spreads can gap through strikes
- Better strategy: Stay flat or use iron condors far OTM

‚ùå **Very Low IV Environments**
- Example: VIX < 10
- Premiums too small to be worth risk
- Better strategy: Wait for IV to expand
