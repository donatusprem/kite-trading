## ðŸŽ¯ Option Positions (Most Important!)

### **ATM - At The Money**
**What it means:** When the option strike price is equal (or very close) to the current stock/index price.

**Example:**
- NIFTY is at 25,935
- Your strike is 25,950
- **Status: ATM** (just 15 points away)

**Why it matters:** ATM options have the most time value and are most sensitive to price movements.

---

### **ITM - In The Money**
**What it means:** When your option has intrinsic value (it would make money if you exercised it right now).

**For Call Options:** Stock price is ABOVE your strike
**For Put Options:** Stock price is BELOW your strike

**Example:**
- VEDL is at â‚¹690.15
- Your call strike is â‚¹685
- **Status: ITM by â‚¹5.15** (profit zone!)

**Why it matters:** ITM options have real value. The deeper ITM, the more they move with the stock price.

---

### **OTM - Out of The Money**
**What it means:** When your option has NO intrinsic value (would be worthless if exercised now).

**For Call Options:** Stock price is BELOW your strike
**For Put Options:** Stock price is ABOVE your strike

**Example:**
- NIFTY is at 25,935
- Your strike is 25,950
- **Status: OTM by 15 points** (needs to go up)

**Why it matters:** OTM options are cheaper but riskier. They lose value fast as expiry approaches.

## ðŸ“Š Technical Analysis Terms

### **VWAP - Volume Weighted Average Price**
**What it means:** The average price weighted by volume. Shows where most trading happened.

**How to use:**
- Price above VWAP = Bullish
- Price below VWAP = Bearish

**Why it matters:** Institutional traders use it to get good entry prices.

---

### **POC - Point of Control**
**What it means:** The price level with the HIGHEST trading volume. Where most traders agreed on value.

**How to use:** Strong support/resistance level.

**Example:** If POC is at 25,900, NIFTY will likely bounce or reject there.

---

### **VA - Value Area**
**What it means:** The price range where 70% of trading volume occurred.

**Components:**
- **VAH** (Value Area High): Top of the range
- **VAL** (Value Area Low): Bottom of the range

**Why it matters:** Price tends to stay within the Value Area. Breaking out is significant.

---

### **HVN - High Volume Node**
**What it means:** Price levels with lots of trading activity (strong support/resistance).

**Example:** If 25,900 has HVN, expect the price to bounce there.

---

### **LVN - Low Volume Node**
**What it means:** Price levels with little trading activity (price moves through quickly).

**Example:** If 25,850 has LVN, price won't stay thereâ€”it'll gap through.

## ðŸ“… Time Frame Analysis

### **HTF - Higher Time Frame**
**What it means:** Longer-term charts (Daily, Weekly, Monthly).

**Use:** To see the big picture trend.

---

### **LTF - Lower Time Frame**
**What it means:** Shorter-term charts (5-min, 15-min, 1-hour).

**Use:** To find precise entry points.

---

### **TF - Time Frame**
**What it means:** The period each candlestick represents.

**Examples:**
- 1D TF = Each candle = 1 day
- 15M TF = Each candle = 15 minutes

## ðŸ”„ Market Structure Terms

### **BOS - Break of Structure**
**What it means:** When price breaks through a previous high/low, showing trend continuation.

**Bullish BOS:** Price breaks above previous high
**Bearish BOS:** Price breaks below previous low

---

### **CHoCH - Change of Character**
**What it means:** When price action changes, signaling potential trend reversal.

**Example:** Uptrend making lower highs = CHoCH (trend might reverse)

---

### **FVG - Fair Value Gap**
**What it means:** A price gap caused by imbalance (big candle with no overlap). Price often returns to fill it.

**Why it matters:** FVGs act as magnetsâ€”price comes back to fill them.

## ðŸ“ˆ Indicators

### **EMA - Exponential Moving Average**
**What it means:** Average price giving more weight to recent prices (faster than SMA).

**Common EMAs:**
- 20 EMA: Short-term trend
- 50 EMA: Medium-term trend
- 200 EMA: Long-term trend

**How to use:** Price above EMA = Bullish, below = Bearish

---

### **SMA - Simple Moving Average**
**What it means:** Average price over a period (all prices weighted equally).

**Example:** 50 SMA = Average of last 50 days

---

### **ATR - Average True Range**
**What it means:** Measures volatility (how much price moves on average).

**High ATR:** Big price swings (more risk, wider stops needed)
**Low ATR:** Small price movements (less risk, tighter stops)

**Use for:** Setting stop loss distances based on volatility

---

## ðŸ“Š Volatility

### **VIX - Volatility Index**
**What it means:** "Fear gauge" measuring expected market volatility.

**Levels:**
- **VIX < 15:** Low volatility (calm market)
- **VIX 15-20:** Normal volatility
- **VIX > 20:** High volatility (nervous market)
- **VIX > 30:** Extreme fear (crash potential)

**Current:** VIX = 11.67 (very calm!)

**Why it matters:**
- Low VIX = Good for call buyers (less premium decay)
- High VIX = Good for option sellers (high premiums)
